I wrote a year ago that a Cathedral Culture is “one built for the long haul.” It took over six centuries for the world famous Cologne Cathedral to be completed, and most Christian cathedrals took just as long. A Cathedral Culture rests on four pillars: currents or long term trends, not short term waves; mission values for success; relationships first, last, and always; and measuring what matters. With strong pillars, companies are able to withstand short term crises and make lasting change.

**What did I think would happen**? Companies and organizations with rich and built-to-last Cathedral Cultures would navigate the complexity, uncertainty, and ambiguity of the pandemic. I thought that Peter Drucker’s famous saying “culture eats strategy for breakfast” would hold true, particularly when managers and leaders had no playbook to consult for guidance.

**What happened?** My prediction turned out to be true. I watched banks go down the rabbit-hole of the PPP and those with clear cultures and values did well; opportunistic banks often got burned. Companies with Cathedral Cultures teamed with their employees to work through unique challenges such as remote work, child care, and home schooling. Cut and run cultures allowed each of these issues to fester and become crises. This has been critically obvious in looking at my home industry of higher education: universities that made decisions based on their core values thrived, those that acted expediently have struggled. Some students have benefited, others have not.

**So What?** What’s the **bottom line**? In the volatile, uncertain, complex, and ambiguous (VUCA) world of COVID-19, no map proved more valuable that a strong sense of mission and purpose, and no compass more useful than values. If you have a culture that builds on the platform I noted a year ago, continue to nourish it. Keep it healthy and strong. If you don’t have a Cathedral Culture, start building one today! We may be on the downward side of COVID-19, but the world will only become more VUCA in the months and years ahead.
