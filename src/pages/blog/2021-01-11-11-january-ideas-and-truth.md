The events of this week have weighed heavily on my mind and heart. I was just about to dive into an afternoon of writing when my news feed flashed with a headline that Congress had suspended its work on the Electoral College certification vote because protestors had entered the capital. I spent the rest of the day, as many of you did, watching the horrific and unique events unfold. I wondered if this was 2021 or 1856, when southern Representative Preston Brooks savagely caned abolitionist Senator Charles Summer on the floor of the US Senate. Or maybe April of 1861 when South Carolina rebels fired on Fort Sumpter and began the Civil War.

As I pondered writing this post and whether I should veer from my topic of alternative energy, I spoke with my daughter. She reminded me that human effort and energy is the most potent and powerful form of energy that drives our economy and our society. Writing about the source of that human energy would be very appropriate for this week’s post, and I want to focus on two ultimate sources of energy: Ideas and Truth.

**Ideas: The power of meaning**

Some very powerful ideals lay at the core of Wednesdays’ events, both the virulent storming of the Capital and the Congressional response: the idea of freedom as the defining characteristic to a morally good life. The thugs and goons who stormed the Capital, and many of those supporting them, saw their freedoms and privileges under siege. They became so totally focused on their vision of freedom—one untethered by legal, moral, or spiritual obligations to other individuals and the collective whole—that they were willing to desecrate the foundation of that freedom, in order to preserve their own vision. In their misguided and whipped up rage, they distorted the righteous idea of freedom and human liberty, a “more perfect union” to phrase Lincoln.

I was so impressed by the response of Senator James Lankford of Oklahoma, shortly after the Senate reconvened. He acknowledged that many of his constituents had concerns about the veracity of claims of election security, but they wanted those claims debated and deliberated through the higher faculty of human reason and civil discourse, not through the rage on an inflamed mobocracy. Lankford, a Baptist minister by vocation, had a very different idea of human freedom and liberty. Freedom from the tyranny of passion and violence as a method of problem solving.

**Bottom line:** Ideas matter because they provide the most basic fuel for human endeavors. History has shown that the ideals of reasoned discourse and living in unity and peace are the most powerful energy humans can provide, more so than hate, selfishness, and division.

**Truth: the power of clarity and reality**

The lies and falsehoods that fueled the insurrection have been on stark display over the past years, months, weeks, and days. Lies and falsehoods are, simply put, stories and narratives that don’t conform to verifiable reality. A deep consideration of facts and data indicate an election—at all levels of government—that was both free and fair. It seems false that Mike Pence would move from being a beloved conservative to a pedophile and member of the “deep state” within a matter of hours, or that Mitch McConnell has been a puppet of the Chinese Communist party for many years, or that Hugo Chavez corrupted an election that would take place years after his death. The power of these lies lays in their ability to provide the cold comfort of grievance, powerlessness, and victimization. That road appeals to many because it absolves them of responsibility for their lives. The problem with lies and falsehood is that they enslave us, removing the fuel for meaningful and positive action.

I loved Mitt Romney, a beacon of truth throughout this entire episode, and his comment that if you really want to help people, tell them the truth. In this case, the truth is that Joe Biden and Kamala Harris won the US Presidential Election. It was neither fraudulent nor “stolen.” How do we know? Because claims to the contrary fail in courts of law and the court of reason. The truth was that the Trumpian vision for America lost in the court of electoral balloting, and to claim otherwise is simply wrong. Romney knew, as most of us who follow truth also know, that truth can be hard at times. No one likes to lose, but real growth and progress only comes as we accept and live in truth. While lies enslave and demean our human energy, truth creates a sanctuary that ennobles us and sets us free.

**Bottom line:** Truth is the only fuel that leads to human growth and progress. Living in truth is not always convenient, and not always pleasant. The fuel of truth, however, is the purest and best form of energy to motivate human action.

This month’s theme is alternative energy. By that I thought I meant new and emerging forms of energy, such as solar, wind, or hydrogen. Alternative really just means different, and what we saw in sharp relief this week were two alternative sources of human energy. One source burned hot and fast, fueled by a selfish vision of freedom and the transitory value of lies. The other source burns brightly and, history has proven, far longer. That source is a set of ideas about men and women living together in peace and harmony, fueled by a correct and real assessment of where we are on that journey. The truth is that America has work to do to make a vision of all people living together in equality, mutual respect, and peace. The ideal, however, is that we can get there.

God bless America, and each individual American.
